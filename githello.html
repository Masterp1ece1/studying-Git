Git is used in most companies where a project has at least two developers:
  1. A new person joins the company and clones the project repository on the desktop.
  2. Receives a task, creates a new branch, and writes code.
  3. When everything is ready, submits a pull request to the master branch:
  4. Other developers review the code, leave comments, and point out errors.
  5. The new person refines the code, updates the master branch, and moves on to the next task.
